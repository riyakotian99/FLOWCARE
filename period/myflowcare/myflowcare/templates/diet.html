1. Diet for Weight Loss

  • High protein foods: Eggs, chicken, fish, tofu, Greek yogurt. Helps maintain muscle and reduces cravings.
  • Complex carbs: Brown rice, oats, quinoa, whole wheat bread. Avoid refined sugar.
  • Fiber-rich foods: Vegetables, fruits (berries, apples), legumes. Helps digestion and satiety.
  • Healthy fats: Olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds.
  • Avoid: Sugary drinks, fried foods, processed snacks.
  • Tip: Eat smaller meals every 3–4 hours to maintain blood sugar.

2. Diet for Weight Gain

  • Protein-rich foods: Eggs, dairy, chicken, lentils, paneer.
  • Complex carbs: Sweet potatoes, oats, whole grains, brown rice.
  • Healthy fats: Nuts, seeds, peanut butter, olive oil.
  • Calorie-dense snacks: Smoothies with milk, banana, and nuts.
  • Frequent meals: 5–6 small meals instead of 2–3 large ones.
  • Tip: Combine strength training with diet for muscle gain.

3. Diet for PCOS/PCOD

  • Low GI foods: Whole grains, oats, barley, brown rice.
  • Protein: Fish, chicken, tofu, legumes.
  • Fiber: Vegetables (broccoli, spinach), fruits (berries, apple).
  • Healthy fats: Avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds.
  • Avoid: Sugar, refined carbs (white bread, cakes), excessive dairy if acne is a problem.
  • Spices: Cinnamon and turmeric help with insulin sensitivity.
  • Tip: Regular meals + exercise improve hormone balance.

4. General Nutrition Tips for Women’s Health

  • Iron-rich foods: Spinach, beans, red meat, dates to prevent period fatigue.
  • Calcium & Vitamin D: Milk, yogurt, cheese, sunlight exposure.
  • Magnesium & Potassium: Nuts, bananas, leafy greens reduce cramps & bloating.
  • Hydration: Drink 2–3 liters of water daily.
  • Avoid excessive caffeine & alcohol: They can worsen PMS symptoms.

5. Extra Information

  • Exercise: Light cardio, yoga, and strength training improve weight management and PCOS symptoms.
  • Sleep: 7–8 hours helps regulate hormones and cravings.
  • Mood & PMS: Dark chocolate, chamomile tea, and magnesium-rich foods help.
  • Supplements (if needed): Omega-3, vitamin D, inositol (especially for PCOS), but consult a doctor first.
